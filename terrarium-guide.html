üè° What is a Terrarium?

A terrarium is a mini ecosystem of plants housed in a clear container. Think of it as a tiny garden that practically takes care of itself! Perfect for college students, busy professionals, or anyone living in a small space.

Why Make a Terrarium?

  • Low Maintenance: Water every 2-3 weeks
  • Space-Saving: Perfect for dorm rooms and apartments
  • Aesthetically Pleasing: A living piece of art
  • Stress Relief: Connect with nature indoors

1 Create the Drainage Layer

Why this matters: Drainage prevents root rot by allowing excess water to drain away from your plants' roots.

Instructions: Add 1-2 inches of small rocks or pebbles to the bottom of your container. This creates a reservoir for excess water.

Use pebbles that are roughly the same size for even drainage distribution.
üì∏ Close-up: Rocks being poured into clear container with 1-2 inch depth marked

2 Add Activated Charcoal

Why this matters: Activated charcoal filters the water, prevents bacterial growth, and keeps your terrarium smelling fresh.

Instructions: Add a thin layer (about 1/2 inch) over the rocks. Note: This step is optional but highly recommended for closed terrariums.

üì∏ Image: Charcoal layer being added with clear distinction between rock and charcoal layers

3 Add Potting Soil

Why this matters: This is where your plants will live and get the nutrients they need to thrive.

Instructions: Add 2-3 inches of potting soil, depending on the size of your plant roots. Use a spoon to make small holes where you'll place each plant.

Gently pat down the soil to remove air pockets, but don't pack it too tightly.
üì∏ Photo: Soil being added with spoon, depth marked | Second image showing prepared holes

4 Plant Your Plants

Instructions: Carefully remove plants from their containers, gently loosen the roots, and place them in the prepared holes. Pat soil around the base to secure them.

Choosing the Right Plants:

üåµ Open Terrariums

Best for: Low humidity environments

Plants: Succulents, cacti, air plants

Container: No lid or wide opening

üåø Closed Terrariums

Best for: High humidity environments

Plants: Ferns, moss, fittonias

Container: Sealed or nearly sealed lid

Start with 2-3 small plants. It's better to leave room for growth than to overcrowd!

Basic Care Instructions:

Common Problems & Solutions:

üü° Yellowing Leaves

Cause: Overwatering
Solution: Let soil dry out. Reduce watering frequency. Remove any dead leaves.

üü§ Brown, Crispy Leaves

Cause: Underwatering or too much direct sun
Solution: Water lightly and move to shadier spot.

üçÑ Mold or Fungus

Cause: Too much moisture
Solution: Remove lid, increase air circulation, reduce watering. Remove affected areas with clean tweezers.
